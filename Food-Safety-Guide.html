Food Safety Guide

Sandwich Preparation, Storage & Delivery for Volunteers
Know the Drill, Always Chill

Why This Matters

TSP serves children, seniors, people who are sick or immunocompromised, and others at higher risk of foodborne illness — many of whom have limited access to healthcare. Our recipients are more vulnerable to foodborne illness than the average population, so we must keep sandwiches and ingredients out of refrigeration for as little time as possible. Deli meat must always be kept at or below 39 °F.

39 °F or below
SAFE — Refrigerator temperature
Above 39 °F
DANGER ZONE — Bacteria multiply rapidly

Step 1

Shopping for Ingredients

  • Transport meat and cheese in coolers with ice. Go directly home or to the office and immediately refrigerate.
  • Purchase the same quality ingredients that you would feed your own family!
  • Do not use over-the-counter deli sliced meats — use packaged meat only (with preservatives).
  • The "use by" date for all ingredients must be at least 7 days after drop-off.
Step 2

Making & Storing Sandwiches

  • All hair must be restrained — use a ponytail holder, hairnet, or hat. Pin back bangs. Beard nets are required for facial hair.
  • Wash hands with soap and warm water — do not use hand sanitizer. Put on gloves and change them if you touch anything other than food.
  • Keep products with nuts away from the deli prep area.
  • Clean and cover all counters. Lay food-safe table coverings once participants are at the table and ready to prepare sandwiches.
  • Keep meat and cheese refrigerated at all times. Remove one package of meat and cheese per table at a time.
  • Place a completed label on the loaf bag.
  • Immediately return completed loaves to the refrigerator before starting another loaf. All loaves must be at refrigerator temperature before packing in coolers.
  • Never overpack the refrigerator. Air must circulate.
No hand sanitizer — soap and water only!
Step 3

Transporting Sandwiches

  • For delivery, use coolers with ice packs.
  • Sandwiches must be chilled in the refrigerator to below 39°F before being placed in coolers.
  • Never put warm sandwiches into a cooler. Coolers maintain temperature — they do NOT make things colder.
  • Close coolers immediately to keep sandwiches below 39°F.
  • Use the correct label at the host home. Labels are mandatory.
Do NOT freeze sandwiches. In hot weather, a brief 15–20 min freezer chill is OK — but never long enough to freeze.
Remember: Coolers keep cold food cold — they don't make warm food cold.

WHEN IN DOUBT, THROW IT OUT!

If you're unsure whether food has been properly handled or stored, it's always safer to discard it.
