Introduction

Welcome to The Sandwich Project

Thank you for volunteering with The Sandwich Project (TSP). Whether you are making sandwiches, hosting a collection site, or delivering meals, you play a critical role in helping us feed our community with dignity and care.

This handbook is organized in two parts: first, food safety principles that apply to everyone; then, step-by-step instructions for your specific role.

Who We Serve

We serve children and students, veterans, seniors, low-income working individuals and families, and people living without permanent housing. Many also face additional challenges such as health conditions, disabilities, limited transportation, and no healthcare insurance.

Food Safety for All

The Cold Chain & Two-Hour Rule

Why Food Safety Matters More for TSP

Many of our recipients — children, seniors, pregnant women, immunocompromised individuals — are more vulnerable to foodborne illness, and many have limited access to healthcare.

The Two-Hour Rule

39 F
Highest Safe Temp
Above this, bacteria begin growing.
80 F
Absolute Limit
Must discard if reached.

Deli meat and cheese may only be above 39 F for a total of two hours, cumulatively — the time adds up across the entire process.

Re-cooling does NOT kill bacteria. If food has been above 39 F for two hours total, discard it.
Food Safety for All

Refrigeration & Cooler Protocols

Refrigeration Standards

Temperature

34 - 38 F consistently.

Air Circulation

Don't block fans. Don't overfill.

Door Discipline

Open briefly only. Temps rise fast.

Cooler Safety

Coolers are NOT refrigerators. They preserve cold temperatures but do not lower them.
Coolers Work For
  • Getting ingredients home from store
  • Transporting pre-chilled sandwiches
  • Maintaining safe temps up to 1 hour
Coolers Do NOT Work For
  • Cooling warm food down
  • Long-term storage
  • Making warm food safe again
Pack Quickly, Keep in Shade

Close cooler between trips to fridge.

Ice Packs Between Rows

All sandwiches in contact with ice.

Pack Full

Less air space = stays colder longer.

Keep Lid Closed

Do not open during transport.

CRITICAL: Keep coolers in the vehicle cabin (A/C) — never in the trunk.

Approved Ingredients

Deli
  • White wheat bread
  • Prepackaged turkey or chicken
  • American cheese (real — not Kraft Singles)
PB&J
  • White wheat bread
  • Smooth peanut butter only
  • Strawberry or grape jelly only (not jam)

Working in Batches

  • One loaf at a time — only one package of meat + cheese from fridge
  • Return immediately to fridge before starting next loaf
  • Teams: divide — some assemble, others pack and refrigerate
  • Keep peanut butter/nut products away from deli ingredients. Separate knives for jelly and PB.

Part 4
Driver
Guidelines
As a driver, you are the final link in the cold chain. Your job is to get sandwiches from host homes to recipient organizations quickly, safely, and at the right temperature.
Drivers

Driver Protocols

Before Your First Delivery

Vehicle Release Form

Required before first delivery. Contact Jordan at 770-789-7329 if you haven't received one.

Delivery Partners

We deliver exclusively to 501(c)(3) organizations. Never directly to individuals.

Transport Protocol

  • Pre-cool your vehicle with A/C before loading
  • Verify everything is cold — if cooler feels warm, do not transport. Contact host.
  • Ice packs between loaves and on top. Pack tightly, close lids immediately.
  • Vehicle cabin only — never in the trunk
  • Drive directly to delivery location — no stops
  • Refrigerate immediately upon arrival at recipient organization
  • Communicate any delays to the recipient organization right away

Frequently Asked Questions

Temperature & Safety
Q: How can I keep deli sandwiches safe during preparation?
Get deli meat back into the refrigerator as quickly as possible at each step.
Q: What if I'm not sure how long meat has been out?
When in doubt, throw it out. If you can't confirm less than two hours total, discard it.
Ingredients
Q: What meat should I buy?
Prepackaged deli-sliced turkey or chicken only.
Q: Why not deli-counter sliced?
Prepackaged is sealed in controlled environments, reducing contamination risk from shared equipment.
Q: What cheese is not acceptable?
No individually wrapped slices or processed "cheese product" (e.g., Kraft Singles). Use real sliced cheese.
Q: What peanut butter?
Smooth only. No crunchy, light, or reduced-fat.
Q: What jelly?
Strawberry or grape jelly only. No jam, preserves, or fruit spread.
Q: Can I add condiments or vegetables?
No. Deli: bread, meat, cheese. PB&J: bread, PB, jelly. Condiment packets available via Amazon wishlist.
Shopping & Storage
Q: How do I pick the right expiration date?
Buy the latest available. Drop-off must be at least 7 days before "use by."
Assembly & Packaging
Q: How much meat per sandwich?
Minimum 2 oz (56g). Thinner slices often need 3-4 slices.
Q: How many cheese slices?
Two: one on each side of the meat.
Q: Peanut butter on both sides?
Yes. 1.5 tbsp smooth PB on both slices, 2 tsp jelly on one.
Q: How do I bag them?
Each in its own bag, squeeze air out, seal. Stack in original bread bag, twist tie.
Q: Can I tape the bread bag?
No — twist ties only. Extras at your host home.
